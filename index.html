From Comfortable Discomfort

What it argues

Comfort is necessary, but too much of it can quietly flatten attention, courage, range, and the mind’s habit of adaptation.

What it covers

Cognitive aging, neuroplasticity, movement, friendship, chosen difficulty, second acts, and how AI can either deepen thought or sedate it.

Who it is for

Readers interested in philosophy, self-development without cliché, modern psychology, and practical ways to stay mentally alive.

AI Should Not Make Us Mentally Softer

A compact version of the argument for readers who want the central idea first.

Open the Medium article →

Most people are not ruined by dramatic catastrophe. More often they are softened by what looks, from the outside, like a very reasonable life.

Things get cleaner. More efficient. Less embarrassing. Less physically demanding. Less cognitively expensive. A person calls it stability. Friends call it maturity.

The point is not to glorify pain. The point is to remember that the human system is adaptive. Whatever it is no longer asked to do, it gradually stops preparing for.
